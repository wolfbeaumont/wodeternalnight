A

Alastor
These are secret agents of the Inner Circle, immune from prosecution by Princes for their actions as they spy on local cities and hunt criminals from the 'Red List'.

Alleycat
A younger Cainite term for a Cainite with no permenant haven, moving night after night, synonymous with Footpad.

Amaranth
Another term for diablerie, when a Cainite drains another's vitae and very soul for their power.

Amria
A Daennosi curse or violent oath. Taken from Amriat, the name of the Cainite in the First City who forced Daenna, the mother of all Daennos to abandon her husband and home.

Anarch
A member of the Anarch Movement or simply a Cainite who opposes the tyranny of the elders.

Anarch Movement, The
Ad-hoc groups of Cainites, that refuse to join a particular sect, and so establish an anarch state in their domain. In reality they fall under the purview of the Camarilla.

Ancilla
A Cainite who has proven themselves, they are socially regarded as between an Elder and a Neonate (plural ancillae).

Angustri
A Daennosi ring, though typically one with magical properties.

Antediluvian
A Cainite of the Third Generation, typically a clan founder.

Antitribu
These are Cainites who have rejected the sect that the majority of their clan is a part of, sometimes they form new bloodlines.

Archbishop
The Cainite ruler of a domain aligned with the Sabbat, they answer to the Cardinals and Regent.

Archon
Envoys of the Justicars, to enact their will.

Assamites, The
A Cainite clan of assassins, with a penchant for blood rituals.

Autarkis
A Cainite divorced from Cainite society, either unnoticed or too powerful to act against.

Awakened, The
The term Magi use to refer to themselves.

B

Baba
"Old woman". A A Daennosi term of respect.

Banker
A younger Cainite term for a Cainite who drinks blood from a blood bank or hospital reserves.

Banking
A younger Cainite term for the practice of drawing blood from blood banks and hospital reserves.

Baron
The Cainite ruler of an Anarch city.

Barrens, The
Areas of a city unfit for life, ie. graveyards, abandoned buildings, industrial zones etc.

Bater
A A Daennosi term meaning "so be it", typically used to conclude a deal or arrangement of some kind.

Baxt
A Daennosi expression for "good luck".

Beast, The
The Beast is within every Cainite, hungry for blood, and Cainites must wrestle with their Beast nightly. Provoked too far, the Beast will induce Frenzy.

Becoming, The
The moment a Cainite crosses over from being a fledging to a neonate, when their Sire deems them ready and the domain's ruler approves. It is also known as the Change

Bishop
Servants of a domain's Archbishop, they are either in charge of a specific aspect of Cainite life, or are a more general spiritual leader. Some Sabbat domains have councils of Bishops instead of Archbishops.

Black Hand, The
A faction within the Sabbat and a mistaken misnomer for the Sabbat as a whole, used by the Camarilla.

Blister
A Cainite who has contracted a mortal disease and spreads it every time they feed.

Blood
A Cainite's heritage.

Blood Bond
Once a Cainite's blood has been drunk three times, the one who drank becomes enslaved to the Cainite's will. It works on both humans and Cainites.

Blood Doll
A mortal who freely gives their blood to a Cainite, some gaining a perverse satisfaction from it, even actively seeking Cainites out.

Bloodline
A Cainite's heritage or a pedigree of Cainites similar to a clan but who cannot trace their creation back to the Third Generation.

Blood Oath
Another term for Blood Bond.

Book of Nod, The
Essentially, the Cainite bible, it chronicles the story of Caine, the first of their kind, after he was turned into a Cainite. Only fragments remain, more myth than fact.

Boon
The currency of Cainite society, in principal owing a favour to another, to be traded as desired.

Brujah, The
A Cainite clan of warrior philosophers turned rebels and criminals, ready to frenzy at a moment's notice.

Bujo
An elaborate Daennosi con on a gaje.

Butterfly
A Cainite who mingles amidst mortal high society, feeding exclusively on them.

C

Caine
Believed to be the first Cainite.

Cainite
A common term for a Cainite amoungst their own kind.

Caitiff
Clanless Cainites, orphans of Cainite society with a high Generation, diluting their clan's characteristics beyond recognition.

Camarilla, The
A sect of genteel Cainites that seek to co-exist with mortals, hidden amongst them, following the Traditions.

Canaille
The mass of humanity, especially the uncultured or unsavory, viewed as a source of sustenance.

Cardinal
Sabbat leaders of massive geographic regions, they answer only to the Regent. They are responsible for directing the Great Jyhad.

Casanova
A younger Cainite term for a male Cainite who seduces victims to feed on them, typically wiping their memory afterwards.

Cauchemar
An elder Cainite term for a Cainite who feeds exclusively on sleeping victims.

Chantry
A communal cultic haven of the Tremere.

Childe
The progeny of a Cainite (plural childr).

Children of Haqim
A Cainite nickname for the Assamites.

Clan
The Cainite's are divided into a number of clans with different characteristics and agendas.

Code of Milan, The
An agreed upon set of principles and rules that the Sabbat live by.

Conclave
These are events that can only be called by a Camarilla Justicar, either due to pressing serious issues or to regularly address local policy. Once a Conclave has been called any grievances can be brought to it by local Cainites.

Consanguineous
Literally means "of the same blood", it is a term referring to lineage.

Coterie
A small group of Cainites united by a common cause or goal, the Sabbat call them Packs.

Court
A social grouping and/or place where the local Camarilla Prince holds meetings of policy etc.

Cunctator
A Cainite who avoids killing when feeding.

D

Dae
Daennosi word for Mother.

Daebara
Daennosi word for the female head of a local family.

Daebaro
Daennosi word for the male head of a local family.

Damned, The
Cainites as a whole.

Deltumnimos
A magical Daennosi potion that expands the abilities of the mind, literally meaning "God's embrace".

Dhartime
A Daennosi touched by, or "claimed by" the element of Earth.

Diablerie
The act of exsanguinating another Cainite to the point of True Death, shunned by some sects and encouraged by others.

Diklo
A heavy Daennosi scarf, typically weighted and used as a weapon.

Dilo
A Daennosi word for a fool or imbecile.

Diocese
A place or social group where Sabbat packs meet to discuss matters of local policy etc.

Discipline
Cainite powers.

Domitor
A Ghoul's master or mistress.

Donor
A younger Cainite term for a vessel, typically human.

Dook
A Daennosi word for the Sight, or magic in general.

Draba
Daennosi items of magical power, typically amulets.

Drabarne
Daennosi mages skilled at creating draba.

Ductus
Sabbat pack leaders (plural ducti), an honorific title for what is essentially a gang leader.

Dukkerin
The Daennosi art of fortune-telling or using the Sight.

E

Elder
A vampire that has lived at least two centuries.

Elysium
A sanctuary for Cainites where violence is not permitted, though the Sabbat have less respect for Elysium. It is usually established in places of culture such as a museum or opera house etc.

Embrace, The
The act of exsanguinating a human and giving them Cainite blood in turn, being the change into a Cainite.

F

Falaqi
A local cell of Assamites.

Farmer
A term of mockery by elder Cainites for a Cainite who refuses to feed on humans, feeding on animals instead.

Fera, The
Shapeshifters, this is the collective term for all of the different sub-species.

Fetter
A place, person or object that ties a spirit to the shadowlands and prevents them from moving on.

Fief
A sarcastic term used by younger Cainites to refer to a Cainite's domain or claim to one, most commonly used in reference to a Prince.

Final Death, The
When a Cainite ceases to exist, crossing into the Underworld, forever.

Fledgling
A newly created Cainite, still under their sire's protection.

Followers of Set, THe
A Cainite clan that worships a chthotic god and seeks out secret places, protecting ancient artefacts.

Footpad
An elder Cainite term for a Cainite who feeds from derelicts and other low hanging fruit in society. They may not have permenant havens.

Frenzy
When a Cainite loses control and the Beast takes over, a complete state of animal ferocity and madness.

G

Gaje
A non-Daennosi human.

Gangrel, The
A beastial Cainite clan, that prefers the wilds over cities.

Garou, The
A sub-species of the Fera, they are otherwise known as werewolves, shapeshifting into the forms of wolves.

Gehenna
The Cainite belief in the end of days, when the first generations of Cainites will rise again and devour their kind.

Generation
Usually used to refer to how far removed a Cainite is from Caine himself, by number of generations, with Cainites growing weaker with each higher generation.

Gentry
An elder Cainite term for a Cainite who preys on victims at bars, clubs and other red-light district establishments.

Ghoul
A human or animal minion, that has been given Cainite blood without being drained.

Giorgio
A non-Daennosi in general.

Giovanni, The
An insular Cainite clan who practice necromancy and have a talent for global finances.

Golconda
A mythic state of being for Cainites, those who have achieved Golconda are said to no longer need to feed, having vanished their Beast and it's hunger.

H

Harpy
These are Cainites within the Camarilla who set trends in various tastes and topics.

Haven
A Cainite's home, where they retreat during the day.

Head
A Cainite who feeds on drunk or drugged people (see Lush) but is usually pre-fixed by their poison of choice, ie. methhead or boozehead etc.

Hound
They work for Sheriffs within the Camarilla, appointed by either the Sheriff or Prince.

Humanitas
The extent to which a Cainite maintain's their humanity.

Hunger, The
A Cainite's urge to feed on blood, it is their primal drive.

Hunter
A mortal who hunts the supernatural.

I

Inconnu, THe
An enigmatic Cainite sect dedicated towards reaching Golconda.

Inner Circle, The
Each clan within the Camarilla has a member on the Inner Circle (usually the clan leader), where they meet once every thirteen years to guide the Camarilla's future.

Ivory Tower, The
A Cainite nickname for the Camarilla.

J

Jastima
A Daennosi touched by, or "claimed by" the element of water.

Jhanaki
A Daennosi touched by, or "claimed by" the element of air.

Juicebag
A younger Cainite derogatory term for a human, indicating they are essentially food.

Justicar
Judges appointed by the Inner Circle, they serve for terms of thirteen years and may only be challenged by another Justicar. They enforce the Traditions when breached on a larger scale and are the Inner Circles agents and enforcers of their will.

Jyhad
An ancient war waged between Cainites, where Elders manipulate the younger generations as pawns against one another.

K

Keeper of Elysium, The
This honorific title of the Camarilla is given to those charged with upholding the customs of Elysium, taking care of the sites for their Princes.

Kindred, The
Meaning Cainite, this term is typically preferred by those belonging to the Camarilla, as a sign of fellowship.

Baron
A contemptuous Cainite term for mortals, with the term "kindred and kine" referring to the world at large, ie. everything.

Kiss, The
The act of a Cainite drinking blood, it causes a state of ecstacy for its victims.

Kuyan
A Daennosi touched by, or "claimed by" the element of fire.

L

Lasombra, The
A proud Cainite clan that commands the shadows and darkness itself.

Leech
A human who drinks Cainite blood but has no master or mistress.

Lextalionis
The Blood Hunt, this is a Cainite decree by Princes for the foulest of crimes, forfeiting the intended's life should they be captured. It is used exceedingly rarely. Diablerie is also pardoned when performed on the outcast.

Lick
A younger Cainite term for a Cainite.

Life, The
A Cainite term for mortal blood, though it is considered conceited.

Lineage
A Cainite's bloodline.

Lupine
One of the Garou, a werewolf.

Lush
A Cainite who feeds on drugged or drunk mortals in order to experience inebriation.

M

Mage
A mortal sorcerer (plural magi).

Malkavians, The
A Cainite clan fractured by madness, each suffering from a form of insanity.

Man, The
The remaining humanity within a Cainite, seperating them from the Beast.

Marhime
A Daennosi term for "unclean or impure". Those who have defiled their blood. The opposite of wuzho.

Masquerade, The
The tradition of hiding the existence of Cainite's from humans.

Methuselah
A Cainite who has existed for a millennium or more, they typically no longer take part in Cainite society.

Mule
A Daennosi term for a ghost or spirit of the dead.

Mule-vi
A Daennosi term for a medium or object capable of reaching the world of the dead.

N

Neonate
A young, recently embraced Cainite.

Nosferatu, The
A hidious Cainite clan that affords the mortal world, living underground in places like sewers where they traffic in secrets.

O

P

Pack
A small group of Sabbat Cainites united under the same cause and goals.

Pack Priest
Each Sabbat pack has a priest in charge of their spiritual needs, second-in-command to the Ductus, they officiate and create rituals for their pack. All packs have at least one pack priest.

Paladin
Handpicked Templars, chosen to senior the most important members of the Sabbat.

Papillon
An elder Cainite term for a domain's red-light district; an area frequented with bars, clubs, brothels, gambling dens etc. Typically the prime hunting ground of many Cainites.

Paramitsha
Daennosi fairy tales.

Patteran
The special Blood Affinity of the Daennosi Phuri Dae family, or an important symbol or other piece of information for them.

Pena
A Daennosi term for "Sister".

Phral
A full-blooded Daennos or true Daennosi.

Phuri Dae
One of the Daennosi families of power. Also any wise woman or woman with magical powers.

Posta
A Daennosi term for a sacrifice, usually ritual in nature.

Prala
A Daennosi term for "Brother".

Praxis
The right for a Cainite Prince to rule a domain or their claim to it. The term also refers to a Prince's matters of policy, edicts etc.

Priscus
These are unofficial advisors (plural prisci) to the local Sabbat authorities.

Prikata
A Daennosi term for "Bad luck".

Primogen
A title for either members of a council that advise the local Prince or simply the eldest and most influencial Cainites of the local domain.

Prince
The Cainite ruler of a domain aligned with the Camarilla.

Progeny
All of a given Caine's childr, collectively.

Pyramid, The
The highly-organised clan hierarchy of the Tremere.

Q

R

Rack, The
A younger Cainite term for a domain's red-light district (see Papillon).

Rake
A younger Cainite term for a habitual Cainite visitor to the Rack.

Ravnos, The
A nomadic Cainite clan that is tied to the Daennos, with a talent for illusions and vices.

Regnant
A Cainite who holds a blood bond over another.

Regent
The sovereign ruler of the Sabbat sect from their capital in Mexico City.

Retainer
A human who serves a Cainite master or mistress.

Ritae
A Sabbat term for a ritual.

Rose Clan, The
A Cainite nickname for the Toreador clan.

S

Sabbat, The
A sect of Cainites dedicated to destroying the ancients, with no regard for mortals.

Sandman
A younger Cainite term for a Cainite that only feeds on sleeping victims (see Cauchemar).

Satarma
A Daennosi term for a star. Also a Daennosi touched by, or "claimed by" the element of spirit.

Scourge
A role within Camarilla whose responsiblity is to purge those of 14th generation or higher, typically in cities infested with thin-bloods.

Sect
A group of Cainites united by a philosophy.

Sheriff
The right hand of a Prince, they are appointed to oversee the enforcement of the Prince's will within a city.

Shilmulo
A Daennosi term for "Cainite". One of the "cold dead".

Shovelhead
A Sabbat term for newly embraced Cainites who are buried and abandoned, awakening with no context as to what they are or what they should do. Typically a tactic used in warzones to create shock troops.

Sire
The one who embraced a Cainite, and is essentially their parent.

Siren
A Cainite who seduces mortals to feed on them.

Slumming
A younger Cainite term for the practice of a Cainite feeding on the homeless and low-hanging fruit of society (see Footpad/Alleycat).

Stalker
A younger Cainite term for a human that hunts Cainites.

Suspire
The rumored epiphany experienced by a Cainite just before they reach the state of Golconda.

Sword of Caine, The
A Cainite nickname for the Sabbat.

T

Talith
A Daennos weapon consisting of a heavy scarf (diklo) weighted and barbed along the end, or the art of using such a weapon.

Tal'Mahe'Ra
A mythic Cainite sect, that spends more in the Underworld than amongst the living.

Tease
A younger Cainite term for a female Cainite that seduces mortals to feed on them (see Casanova).

Templar
Elite bodyguards, appointed to protect senior Sabbat figures, as well as serve them in other duties.

Third Mortal, The
Another term for Caine, the first Cainite.

Thrall
A Cainite under the effects of a blood bond to another Cainite.

Toreador, The
A Cainite clan that lives for sensual pleasure and beauty.

Tremere, The
A Cainite clan with a scorcerous past and a penchant for blood magic.

Tsurara
A new and radical Daennosi family of power.

Tumnimos
A Daennosi term for the Cainite embrace.

7

Turf
A younger Cainite term for someone's domain or an area given to a gang's influence.

Tzimisce, The
An eldrich Cainite clan of the Old World, with little in common with mortals, they manipulate flesh and bones at whim.

V

Vardo
A Daennos wagon, RV camper etc.

Siren
A younger Cainite contemptuous term for a Cainite that feeds exclusively on animals (see Farmer).

Ventrue, The
An entitled Cainite clan that believe in the creed of greed and power.

Vessel
A source of blood for a Cainite, usually a human.

Vitae
A Cainite term for blood.

W

Witch-Hunter
An elder Cainite term for a human that hunts Cainites (see Stalker).

Whelp
A derogatory Cainite term for a young Cainite, originally it was used exclusively for one's own progeny.

Wight
A Cainite term for "human, man or mortal".

Whig
A contemptuous Cainite term for a Cainite who possesses an interest in mortal trends and fashions.

Wretch*
A Cainite who feeds on their own kind whether out of necessity or depravity.

Wuzho
A Daennosi term for "pure or untainted". The opposite of marhime.
